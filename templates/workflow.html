Workflow

Getting Started

A rough overview of moving through your day:

  • Set up daily worksheet
  • Review google sheets for sold cars
  • Review Rapid Recon for what to expect in the basement
  • Bring worksheet to basement and review what's marked for photos
    • If a car is sold, remove the green sign and park the car in an 'exit lane' if possible, making it easier to move your cars needing photos through the lane
    • If a car is marked in 'photos' on Rapid Recon, but lacks a green sign, and looks very clean with paper floor mats, it's probably ready for photos. Check with detail if you're unsure
    • If a car has a green sign but looks very dirty, bring to the attention of detail. If it's a small area or one that won't show in photos, you can photograph it and have them touch it up after. If it's very visible in photos, have them spot clean it first.
    • For all new cars and retired loaners marked for photo, write down the details of the car on the worksheet
  • Take worksheet upstairs and check if we already have photos in the catalog for the new cars and retired loaners
  • Photograph the cars needing pictures
  • Once photographed, car can be moved in Rapid Recon from 'Photos' to 'Frontline'
  • Import the pictures from the card into Lightroom
  • Batch edit the photos, locally adjust as needed, and export the images
  • Export the images and upload to vAuto

Rapid Recon

Rapid Recon is a web based software that both dealerships use to track and communicate about pre-owned cars in inventory. Note that it is only for pre-owned and not new cars. This can be used in conjunction with the google sheets info to help you plan what cars need to be photographed, what cars to expect the next day, and to communicate to others that you've finished photographing a car. vAuto

vAuto is an online software that you will use for uploading photos. Images uploaded here appear on the dealerships websites as well as various third party auto sales websites. It sometimes takes a full day for images to appear on websites outside of vAuto, so if a salesperson needs photos immediately, you will need to send them via email, dropbox, etc. See the steps below for navigating and using vAuto.
